Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a delicious classic roman pasta. It consisists of a silky cheese sauce that is made without cream. It also includes guanciale like amatriciana.

Ingredients

  • Pasta: the most common is spaghetti
  • Parmigiano Reggiano: Pecorino may be substituted
  • Guanciale: an italian cured pork cheek
  • Egg Yolk and Egg White this is what gives the sauce a creamy consistency
  • Salt and Black Pepper: for seasoning

Steps

  1. Finely grate the parmigiana reggiano or pecorino. Whisk together the egg, cheese and pepper in a large bowl. It needs to be a large bowl because the pasta will be stirred into the sauce in the bowl, off the stove, to avoid scrambling the eggs.
  2. Cook pasta. Bring 4 litres (4 quarts) of water to the boil with 1 tablespoon of salt. Cook the pasta per packet directions. It should be firm, not soft, but fully cooked through.
  3. Cook guanciale until golden while the pasta is cooking. You don't need any oil, the guanciale will fry in its own fat.
  4. Transfer into sauce bowl. Tip the hot pasta into the bowl with the egg and use a rubber spatula to scrape out every drop of the guanciale fat into the bowl. That stuff is gold!
  5. Add 1/2 cup pasta cooking water into the bowl.
  6. Mix vigorously with the handle of a wooden spoon, spinning the pasta around, for around 30 seconds to 1 minute. Watch as the watery pale yellow liquid magically transforms into a creamy sauce.
  7. Serve. Your carbonara is complete!
